Hi, I’m Haroula Baliaka

Atmospheric Chemistry • Air Quality • Aerosols

PhD candidate in Environmental Science & Engineering at Caltech. My research focuses on atmospheric composition, particulate matter, and trace-element dynamics, with an emphasis on wildfires, dust, and community-relevant air-quality monitoring.

About

I study atmospheric chemistry and air quality, integrating field measurements, sensor networks, and data analysis to understand aerosol sources, transformations, and exposures.

My work spans wildfire smoke, post-fire dust, trace elements, and multi-instrument observational strategies, with a strong interest in translating science into public-health and policy-relevant insights.

Projects

PHOENIX

A dense, neighborhood-scale air-quality monitoring network supporting post-fire recovery through real-time PM2.5 and PM10 observations.

ASCENT

Integrated aerosol measurements and analysis workflows to characterize sources, dynamics, and exposure patterns across the Los Angeles Basin.

Trace Metals & Source Apportionment

Trace-element enrichment, PMF source apportionment, and multi-instrument mass closure to interpret wildfire plumes and dust events.
